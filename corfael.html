Corfael

Corfael are large green feathered creatures resembling a cross between a cat and a wyvern. They have two hind legs and two wings in the place of forelegs, although the wings can also function as slightly cumbersome legs when Corfael are on the ground. Their tail ends in a tuft of feathers which can be used to aid in steering during flight.

All Corfael wear masks made of bone china; they are an aggressive species and use it as protection during fights and hunts. They are quick to squabble with other members of their community, but these fights are generally low stakes and only intended to assert and reestablish dominance over each other. Corfael keep a close eye on their territory and may react very aggressively if they think someone is harming their home.

Corfael have two sexes and reproduce sexually. They also exhibit sexual dimorphism, with males being slightly smaller than females and having a feathery ridge down their back. Corfael are intensely protective of their families and mate for life, building up large communities of family members. While children may travel away from parents when mature, they will not travel far, and will still see their parents as part of their close family.

Corfael are native to coniferous mountains. Twice a year they migrate to a different mountain in search of a replenished food supply. Corfael hunt any and all nonmagical animals, although they prefer larger game like moose.
